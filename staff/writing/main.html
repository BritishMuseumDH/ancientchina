The Writing chapter examines the nature and uses of writing in ancient China. It also looks at the writing system used in China.

This page briefly outlines the principles behind Chinese writing and language.

The Story traces the discovery of some of the earliest examples of writing from ancient China found on oracle bones. This section could be used to learn about how writing was used in ancient China. It could also be used as an example of how archaeologists and historians can learn about the development of writing systems around the world through accidental discoveries.

The Explore presents examples of writing from ancient China, including oracle bones, inscribed bronze vessels, wooden slips and money. This section could be used to examine how writing was used in ancient China, what types of objects writing appeared on, the importance of writing on particular types of objects and the materials used in writing.

The Challenge presents an opportunity for children to examine some of the symbols that have been found on ancient Chinese objects. They need to match the ancient symbol with the picture of the object it describes. All the symbols used in this game are pictographic. However it needs to be stressed to the children that the Chinese writing system is not solely based on pictographic symbols. They form a very small fraction of Chinese characters.
